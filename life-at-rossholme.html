Life at Rossholme.

In the heart of Nairobi's Kiambiu, a corrugated tin and breeze-block building stands as a beacon of
hope for 525 children.

A Safe Haven

The Kiambiu slum is a challenging environment for a child to grow up in. Rossholme provides a safe, structured space where children aged 3 to 12 can simply be children.

Beyond a robust primary education, we provide two critical meals a day: a free breakfast and a free lunch. For many of our pupils, these are the only meals they will eat all day. Nourishment is the foundation of learning; you cannot teach a
hungry child.

Under the leadership of Headteacher Joyce Aruga, the school has grown from just six children in 2016 to a bustling community of 525 eager learners.

East Africa Children's Project

Charity No. 1136340

We have a close and useful link with East Africa Children’s Project (EACP), a UK-based charity (No 1136340). They’ve supported Rossholme, funded solar panels, and donated money for new locker desks. EACP will now help us with our £25,000 campaign to buy land and property in Kiambiu Slum where Rossholme is. www.eacpcharity.com

EACP’s Chairman, Pieter Lagaay, visited Rossholme in Nairobi and was impressed by Headteacher Joyce Aruga, the school, staff, and pupils and continues to support Rossholme.

Tushinde

"We succeed" in Kiswahili

Providing Family Support To Help Vulnerable Children Thrive

Tushinde Children's Trust, a charity founded by Megan Wright, provides family support to vulnerable children in Nairobi slums. They recently expanded their work to the Kiambiu slum and partnered with Rossholme School to protect child rights. This partnership is crucial for establishing Rossholme School as a charity and providing outreach support to both children and their families. In collaboration with Tushinde, they aim to open a Day Care Centre for younger children, freeing up space at Rossholme School for school-aged children.

Founder Trustee

Judith Webb

Born in Somerset and educated at Rossholme School and Bath College. Commissioned into British Army in 1968; first woman to command all male field force unit 1982. Retired in 1986 to become Head of Rossholme School; Chairman, Council of Order of St John for Somerset 2000-2006; Governor Downs School Wraxall 1996-2006. Closed Rossholme School in 2005 and ran Clocktower Events Ltd, Rossholme Country Holidays and Clocktower Wellness Centre. Married to former Army Officer, Gerry, two adult children. Enjoys politics, travel, skiing, fitness and walking. Always happy to speak about Women on the Warpath and Rossholme in Kenya.

Trustee

Doug Brodie

Based in London, initially from Glasgow, Doug was brought up in early years in Kenya and Amin's Uganda, where his father was one of two Scottish surgeons, and where he remembers school lessons taken sitting on the ground under trees for shade. Via Strathclyde University, the deserts of Saudi Arabia, Kuwait and Yemen, since 1995 he has run a financial planning firm on Chancery Lane in the heart of the legal district, and is passionate about all things outdoors, dogs, rugby and financial education.
